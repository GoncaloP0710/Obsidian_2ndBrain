# Incident Management

IM Phases
- Plan and Prepare
- Detection and Reporting
- Assessment and Decision
- Responses
- Lessons Learnt

## Plan and Prepare

This phase focuses on **proactive planning and preparation** to ensure that the organization is ready to handle incidents effectively when they occur.
- define and document a detailed information security incident management plan, including topics covering communications and information disclosure
- formulate and produce an information security incident management policy

## Detection & Reporting

In this phase, the organization **identifies and reports potential security incidents**. Early detection is crucial to minimize the impact of an incident.
- detect and report the occurrence of an information security event

## Assessment and Decision

In this phase, the Incident Response Team **assesses the incident** to determine its scope, impact, and potential risks. Based on the assessment, they make decisions on the appropriate response.

## Responses

This phase involves **executing the response plan** to **contain, eradicate, and recover** from the incident. The goal is to minimize damage and resume normal operations as quickly as possible.
